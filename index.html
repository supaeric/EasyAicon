Aircon FAQs: prices, sizes and installation in Durban.

The things Durban customers ask us most.

How much does aircon installation cost in Durban?

Installation-only for a standard midwall split unit typically ranges from about R2,500 to R4,500, depending on pipe run length, wall type and unit position. A new 9,000 BTU aircon supplied and installed typically starts from around R8,000, with larger inverter units costing more. Every quote is free, firm and bundled, unit, installation and materials in one price.

How much does an aircon regas cost in Durban?

A regas (refrigerant gas refill) for a home split unit typically costs between R800 and R1,500, including a leak check. If your aircon keeps losing gas there's a leak that should be repaired first, otherwise the gas simply escapes again. We quote the exact price before any work starts.

What size aircon do I need for my room?

As a guide: 9,000 BTU suits a standard bedroom, 12,000 BTU a large bedroom or small lounge, and 18,000–24,000 BTU an open-plan living area or office. Durban's humidity means it's worth sizing up slightly. Send us your room dimensions and we'll recommend the right unit in your free quote.

Inverter vs non-inverter aircon, which is better?

Inverter aircons adjust compressor speed instead of switching on and off, so they typically use 30–50% less electricity, run quieter and hold temperature better, the right choice for rooms you cool daily. Non-inverter units cost less upfront and suit rooms used occasionally. We quote both options so you can compare.

Do I lose the manufacturer warranty if I don't buy from a big retailer?

No, what protects your warranty is certified installation, not where you bought the unit. Every installation we arrange is done by a qualified technician, which keeps your manufacturer warranty fully valid, and workmanship is guaranteed.

Why is my aircon not cooling?

The most common causes are dirty filters, low refrigerant gas from a slow leak, a faulty capacitor, or a failing compressor. Durban's coastal salt air also corrodes outdoor units faster than inland. A technician can usually diagnose the fault on the first visit and quote the repair before doing any work.

How often should an aircon be serviced?

In Durban, every 6 to 12 months. Coastal humidity and salt air cause mould, blocked drains and corrosion faster than inland, so units near the coast benefit from twice-yearly servicing. Regular servicing keeps electricity usage down and prevents expensive repairs.

How long does an aircon installation take?

A standard midwall split installation takes about 3–5 hours, including mounting, vacuum-tested pipework, trunking and commissioning. Multi-unit and commercial installations are scheduled per site, usually with one point of contact and one invoice.
